# Data and content notice

`LICENSE` (MIT) covers the code. This file answers the question MIT does not:
what are the *data* files, and may you redistribute them? Short answer: yes, all
of it.

## The underlying DOL filings

Runway's shortlists are built from US Department of Labor LCA disclosure data,
published here:

<https://www.dol.gov/agencies/eta/foreign-labor/performance>

That data is a work of the United States federal government and is not subject
to domestic copyright protection (17 U.S.C. §105). It is free to use, modify,
and redistribute. Runway does not commit the raw files — `data/raw/` is
gitignored and the pipeline downloads them fresh (see `README.md`, "Repo map").

## The derived artifacts in `web/data/`

`web/data/<role>.{json,csv,provenance.json}` are generated by this repository's
own code (`scripts/build_shortlist.py`) from that public data. They are covered
by the same MIT terms as the code that produces them.

They are **build outputs, not source.** Regenerate them with
`python scripts/run.py --no-fetch` rather than editing them by hand — see
`CONTRIBUTING.md`.

## The curated content

`prompts/recommendations.md`, the `*.standout_paths.json` files, and the site
copy in `web/` are written by hand rather than generated. They are also MIT.
Splitting them onto a separate content license would be more precise and more
friction than a project this size warrants.

## What this is not

Runway is career and portfolio guidance, not immigration legal advice. A
certified LCA filing means an employer sponsored an occupation at a wage level
at some point. It does not mean a role is open, and it does not mean anyone
will sponsor you. The full caveats ship with every role's data and live in one
place in the code — `engine/_util.py`'s `CAVEATS`.
